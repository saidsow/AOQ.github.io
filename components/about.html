Our Journey

"SHOW ME THE MONEY!"

AOQ was born from a profound realization—that the traditional sports management model was failing athletes. Drawing inspiration from the ethos of Jerry Maguire, we recognized that true advocacy means fewer clients, more dedication, and genuine personal investment.

Our founders stepped away from the corporate machine of talent management to create something authentic. Like Jerry's famous mission statement, "The Things We Think and Do Not Say," we built AOQ on the principle that athletes aren't just commodities but people who deserve managers who will work tirelessly on their behalf.

"If this is empty, we're going to look at each other and say 'All right. Who's willing to go out there with me?'"

This line from the film encapsulates our founding spirit—the courage to walk away from the comfort of conventional management to create something more meaningful. At AOQ, we're not just agents; we're advocates who believe in building genuine connections and fighting for our clients' best interests.

Today, we continue to embrace the philosophy that "the key to business is personal relationships." Our success isn't measured by the size of our client roster but by the depth of our commitment to each individual we represent.

"I will not rest until I have you holding a Coke, wearing your own shoe, playing a Sega game featuring you, while singing your own song in a new commercial, starring you, broadcast during the Super Bowl, in a game that you are winning, and I will not sleep until that happens."
— The AOQ Promise

Meet the Team

Matt Aptaker

Matt Aptaker is a dedicated sports professional with a passion for helping athletes achieve their full potential. With a background in sports management and marketing, Matt brings a wealth of experience to the AOQ Sports team.

Matt has worked with numerous athletes and sports organizations, providing strategic guidance and support to help them reach their goals. His expertise in branding and promotion has been instrumental in elevating the profiles of many athletes.

Artak "The Armenian Nightmare" Petrosyan

Artak "The Armenian Nightmare," Petrosyan, is a retired professional mixed martial artist and a former Colorado Combat Club Champion. Competing in the welterweight division. With over 15 years in the sport, he is a seasoned MMA veteran who holds a black belt in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu from Gracie Barra.

Artak began his professional career in regional promotions, quickly making a mark with his dynamic fighting style and resilience in the cage. He secured the Colorado Combat Club Welterweight Championship, and built a reputation as a fierce competitor who always left it all in the cage.

Now retired from active competition, Artak has transitioned his passion for MMA into management and mentorship. Leveraging his years of experience, he is committed to guiding the next generation of fighters, helping them navigate their careers, and achieve success both inside and outside the cage.
